About Me

As an Infocomm CCA member, I have gone to multiple competitions for my CCA, namely using Lego robots or drones and competing against other teams. It has taught me patience with coding as I may have technical oversights sometimes, and collaborative skills as these competitions are a team activity.

In terms of academics, I can do well with mathematics-adjacent topics. I have used my knowledge in trigonometry for a modeling project once. I also have VIA projects for CCA.

In S2 and S3, my CCA mates and I went to Heartbeat Bedok and Hougang Community Centre to host a workshop for children to use the robot parts belonging to the CCA to try robotics, and we were there to stand by and guide them whenever they needed it. I was thus able to demonstrate patience and care for others.

Journey

Participations & Achievements


  1. RCAP Rescue Line

    1st Place 🏅 (w/ Javier Lim and Keyon Weeracondadabaduge)


    Scenario: A disaster has struck, leaving victims in terrain too dangerous for humans to access. Your team must build a fully autonomous robot capable of navigating challenging terrain (including hills, uneven ground, and rubble) without getting stuck. The robot must locate victims, transport them safely to an evacuation point, and continue the mission until all rescues are complete, all without any human intervention.


    Given the rules, I coded the logic that allows the bot to follow green markers at intersections to determine which direction to turn.


  2. Up Your Game is a game development mentorship programme by *SCAPE designed for aspiring developers in Singapore. Open to Singaporeans and PRs aged 16 to 35, the programme offers participants the chance to build their skills and develop their pitches under the guidance of experienced industry mentors.


    Excited by the opportunity, I registered my interest and submitted my game concept to be considered for the programme. The intense three-month programme ran from May to July, and I was thrilled to be accepted and receive guidance from a veteran in the industry. As the youngest participant, I created a sci-fi survival game called Drift In Dread and was mentored by Xuanming Zhou - a veteran Singaporean indie game developer.
